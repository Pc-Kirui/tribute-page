Abraham Lincoln

16th President of the United States

Here's is a timeline of Abraham Lincoln

  • Feb 12, 1809 - Born in Sinking Spring Farm, Kentucky U.S.
  • Education - Lincoln was largely self educated. At age seven he learned to read, and went to school sporadically for a total of less than 12 months by age 15, due to farm chores and splitting rails for fences.
  • As a Teen - Took responsibility in chores and customarily gave his father all earnings outside home until he was 21. At his youth he was an active wrestler and became the county champion at age 21.
  • 1831 and 1832 - Lincoln worked at a general store in New Salem, Illinois. In 1832, he declared his candidacy for the Illinois House of Representatives, but interrupted his campaign to serve as a captain in the Illinois Militia during the Black Hawk War. On returning from the war he acquired the New Salem store and bartending licences together with his friend Berry.
  • 1834 to 1842 - Campaigned as a whig which was a success, and served four terms in the Illinois House of Representatives for Sangamon County. He was admitted to the Illinois bar in 1836, and moved to Springfield and began to practice law under John T. Stuart, Mary Todd's cousin.
  • Novembe 4, 1842 - Married Mary Todd in the Spring Field mansion of Mary's sister,Illinois. Lincoln was an affectionate husband and a father of four sons, only one of whom lived to maturity.
  • 1847 to 1849 - U.S. House of Representatives. On foreign and military policy, Lincoln spoke against the Mexicanâ€“American War.
  • 1854 to 1860 - Republican Politics. Lincoln frequently expressed his moral opposition to slavery in public and private.
  • January 1860 - Lincoln told a group of political allies that he would accept the presidential nomination if offered and, in the following months, several local papers endorsed his candidacy.
  • Feb 27, 1860 - Lincoln gave a speech at Cooper Union, in which he argued that the Founding Fathers of the United States had little use for popular sovereignty and had repeatedly sought to restrict slavery. Lincoln demonstrated intellectual leadership that brought him into contention.
  • November 6,1860 - Lincoln was elected the 16th president. He was the first Republican president and his victory was entirely due to his support in the North and West.
  • 1861 to 1865 - The presidential transition of Abraham Lincoln began when he won the United States 1860 United States presidential election, becoming the president-elect of the United States, and ended when Lincoln was inaugurated at noon on March 4, 1861. As President, he built the Republican Party into a strong national organization. Further, he rallied most of the northern democrats to the union cause. On January 1, 1863, he issued the Emancipation Proclamation that declared forever free those slaves within the Confedaracy. Lincoln won re-election in 1864, as Union military triumphs heralded an end of the war. On March 4, 1865, Lincoln delivered his second inaugural address. In it, he deemed the war casualties to be God's will. The spirit that guided him was that of his Second Inaugral Address now inscribed on one wall of the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C.
  • Friday, April 14, 1865 - Lincoln was assasinated in Washington at Ford's theatre by John Wilke's Booth, an actor, who somehow thought he was helping the south.
